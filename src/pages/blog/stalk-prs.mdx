## "They'll let us know when they struggle"

They won't. Or very few will. This could be for a number of reasons:

- The process to contribute to the design system appears an arduous, political or highly gate kept process.
- They have little capacity or incentive to get involved in design system work since their performance is measured on reaching their team's sprint goal.
- They don't even see their misuse as an issue or struggle unless they are blocked by it i.e. they are unable to hack around it to achieve what they need or find it's too difficult or time consuming to build their own version of the component.

## "It's in the documentation though"

If your users are often asking questions already answered in the documentation, then your documentation is probably shit ğŸ’©. **Yes, documentation needs content designing too.** Just because the information is there, doesn't mean it's been presented in a way that's easily digestible, clear and in the format needed for your users.

There is also such a thing as too much documentation. Reduce the content! **What is actually used and needed here?** Good design is about managing attention.

Engineers are wonderfully lazy ğŸ¦¥ but smart people. They'll do everything they can to not leave their IDE and have to read documentation. **Good design is first intuitive then discoverable**. Don't band aid the issue of bad component API design with more documentation for the component, prioritise the API. Reduce cognitive load needed to use your components APIs. You can do this by leveraging existing and familiar APIs such as HTML attributes and React's children prop for example. Sharing APIs across common components. Don't build these complex monolithic components that have abstracted everything into a list of props. Build composable components where the lower level components can be easily accessed.

## Designers make mistakes too

If you're a product engineer who finds themselves needing to build a slightly different version of a design system component or needing to use a custom value instead of a token to match the designs you were given... push back on the designer. Question them. Why does the design system version of this component not cut it here? Why can't we use the 12px spacing token here instead of a custom 10px here? There might be a good reason for it, there might not be.
